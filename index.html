Press Release · Plaquenil Regulated Access

Sanofi Türkiye and the Ministry of Health propose a Regulated Access Framework for Plaquenil®

A first-of-its-kind Digital Health Pass would guarantee medication continuity for lupus and rheumatoid arthritis patients — even during a public-health emergency.

By the Sanofi Türkiye Government Affairs Office Filed: For Immediate Release

İstanbul — Sanofi Türkiye, in partnership with the Ministry of Health, the Turkish Medicines and Medical Devices Agency (TİTCK), and the Social Security Institution (SGK), today proposes a Regulated Access Framework for hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil®) — the cornerstone therapy for patients living with systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) and rheumatoid arthritis.

The framework is a direct response to the 2020 supply crisis, when global panic-buying following early off-label COVID-19 speculation left more than one in three lupus patients unable to fill their prescriptions. Hydroxychloroquine orders surged more than 260% within a two-week window in March 2020, displacing the chronic patient population whose lives depend on the medicine.

The proposal is built on Türkiye's existing infrastructure — e-Nabız, the İlaç Takip Sistemi (İTS), the Sağlık Uygulama Tebliği (SUT) reimbursement instrument, and the Tıbbi ve Ekonomik Değerlendirme Komisyonu — rather than introducing a new bureaucratic layer. The framework can be codified through a focused amendment, not a new law.

In a public-health crisis, the first people who should be protected are the patients who already depend on the medicine. The Regulated Access Framework ensures this never happens again — and Türkiye is positioned to be the first country to codify it.
— Mehmet Emin Yılmaz, Spokesperson, Sanofi Türkiye Government Affairs

The Framework: Four Pillars

01

Registry

Digital Health Pass

Mandatory ICD-10 verification at every Plaquenil dispensation point, integrated into e-Nabız and the İTS barcode-tracking infrastructure.

02

Reserve

Strategic Stock Buffer

Sanofi maintains a 90-day rolling reserve at one designated pharmacy per district, codified through a focused SUT amendment.

03

Restrict

Crisis-Trigger Lock

Automatic suspension of off-label dispensing during a WHO-declared public-health emergency, lifted only when peer-reviewed clinical evidence justifies it. Off-label use remains available through TİTCK's existing named-patient procedure.

04

Reimburse

SGK Continuity Guarantee

Zero co-pay for registered SLE and rheumatoid arthritis patients; the State recovers costs through documented savings from prevented severe-flare hospitalizations.

The Economic Case

Maintenance therapy for a stable lupus patient costs roughly $1,500 per year. A single severe lupus flare — typically requiring hospitalization or ICU admission — costs approximately $75,000. Preventing one severe flare therefore funds maintenance therapy for fifty patients, a 1-to-50 cost-offset ratio.

Setup costs for the digital infrastructure are estimated at $35–55 million over five years, with a projected payback of 12 to 18 months. Preventing severe flares in just one percent of the SLE population is projected to save the State $30–40 million annually.

The Ask

Sanofi Türkiye formally invites the Ministry of Health to convene a 90-day technical dialogue with TİTCK, SGK and the Tıbbi ve Ekonomik Değerlendirme Komisyonu. The dialogue would produce three concrete outputs: a draft SUT amendment ready for ministerial signature; a joint cost memorandum with break-even calculations and risk allocation; and a pilot plan for İstanbul, Ankara and İzmir with measurable KPIs and exit criteria.

The framework is designed to be reviewed every five years, with a sunset clause to ensure it remains aligned with clinical evidence, market dynamics, and patient need. The Digital Health Pass is shared infrastructure, available equally to generic manufacturers — no extension of market exclusivity is sought or granted.

Safeguards and Stakeholder Voice

The framework will be co-designed with the Turkish Rheumatology Society, the Lupus Hasta Derneği, and the Turkish Pharmacists' Association (TEB). The Tıbbi ve Ekonomik Değerlendirme Komisyonu retains independent oversight of all reimbursement decisions. KVKK and GDPR-compliant data handling is built into the registry from day one.
